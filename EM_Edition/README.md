# EM Edition - The Original Sin Reversal

## Overview
This analysis tracks the historic "Original Sin Reversal" - the structural shift where disciplined Emerging Markets now offer superior fiscal positions and real yields compared to debt-laden G7 nations. We monitor the end of the era where EM countries were forced to borrow in foreign currencies due to weak credibility.

## Key Themes
- **The Credibility Gap**: G7 vs EM real yield divergence showing where capital flows for mathematical reasons
- **The Fiscal Divergence**: Primary surplus trajectories revealing the great role reversal in fiscal discipline
- **Safety Buffer vs Fiscal Ceiling**: EM operating with fiscal discipline while G7 hits debt sustainability limits
- **The New Global Alpha**: Capital flowing to where the math works - disciplined EM over profligate G7

## Key Insights
- EM "Fiscal Adult" group averaging 4.2% real yields vs G7's troubled 0.8%
- US/UK interest-to-revenue ratios breaching dangerous 30% "Fiscal Dominance Zone"
- Countries like Indonesia and Mexico maintaining stable debt-service profiles
- G7 experiencing "Fiscal Hockey Stick" deterioration while EM shows consolidation discipline

## Data Sources
- **G7 Government Bond Yields**: Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED)
- **G7 Inflation Data**: National statistical offices via FRED
- **EM Government Bond Yields**: Multiple sources including Bloomberg, national banks
- **Primary Surplus Data**: IMF, OECD, national treasury departments
- **Real Yield Calculations**: Nominal yields minus inflation rates

## Key Metrics to Monitor
- **Credibility Gap**: EM-G7 real yield spread (target >3.0pp for full reversal)
- **Fiscal Divergence**: EM-G7 primary surplus gap (target >4.0pp for paradigm shift)  
- **Fiscal Dominance Zone**: Countries with interest payments >30% of revenues
- **Safety Buffer**: Real yields >3.0% with positive primary surplus
- **Fiscal Hockey Stick**: Rapid deterioration >1.5pp in short period
